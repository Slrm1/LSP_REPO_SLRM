Honestly, at the beginning of the semester I treated AI like a cheat code for code I did not want to write. Now I reach for it more like Google on steroids: I will ask it to explain something I am fuzzy on, or to help me skim a topic before I dig into the actual reading, or to sanity-check whether I am even asking the right question. It still helps with the tedious stuff sometimes—cleaning up comments, roughing out a test—but I have had enough moments where it sounded confident and was wrong that I do not just paste and submit anymore. I run it, I read the stack trace, I go back to the slides if I need to. Going forward I will probably keep using it that way for research and for getting unstuck, as long as I am the one who actually understands what is on the page when it counts.
